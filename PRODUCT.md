# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Personal/family collectors (e.g. Dario, Papa) managing their own vinyl record collections: cataloguing records, tracking metadata (artist, title, label, year, genre, cover, tracklist), browsing/searching their own and others' collections, scanning barcodes to add new records, checking stats on their collection, and managing who can see their (private) collection.

## Product Purpose

VinylVault is a vinyl record collection manager. It lets a small group of personal users catalogue, browse, and maintain their own record collections with metadata auto-fill (Discogs/MusicBrainz lookup, barcode scanning), and lets them selectively share or keep collections private among the group. Success looks like fast, low-friction logging of a new record and an enjoyable, accurate way to browse what's already in the collection.

## Brand Personality

Warm, tactile, collector-grade. Should feel like flipping through a well-kept record crate, not like managing rows in a spreadsheet: a sense of physical media, craftsmanship, and personal ownership, even though it's a data-driven app.

## Anti-references

Generic SaaS dashboard: gray flat cards, gradient hero-metric tiles, cookie-cutter stat widgets, sterile enterprise-admin aesthetics. The app should not look like a generic CRUD admin panel.

## Design Principles

- Tactile over corporate: surfaces, color, and type should evoke analog/physical media, not generic SaaS chrome.
- Fast logging, easy browsing: the cataloguing and search/browse flows are the core jobs; friction there costs more than friction elsewhere.
- Small-group trust, not anonymous scale: permissions and sharing (public/private collections, grants) should feel personal and clear, not enterprise-style ACLs.
- Data integrity without ceremony: metadata lookup/autofill should feel like a helpful assist, not a separate chore.
- Respect existing data: changes should never require migration of legacy/imported records.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA baseline: sufficient color contrast, full keyboard navigation, screen-reader friendly labels, and standard `prefers-reduced-motion` support. No additional accommodations specified beyond this baseline.
